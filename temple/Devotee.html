Buddhist Celebrations Buddhism celebrates many holidays and festivals throughout the year. These celebrations are observed as a way of honoring important figures, moments, and themes in Buddhist tradition. The annual calendar within Buddhist communities is populated with holidays and festivals for reflection and gathering together. One of the most important Buddhist holidays is Vesak, also known as Buddha Day. It falls on the first full moon in May and commemorates Buddha’s birth, enlightenment, and death.
